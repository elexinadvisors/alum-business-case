Step 1

Market Research & Background

The Australian wellness tourism sector is valued at approximately AUD $14.7 billion (2025) and is projected to grow at ~10% CAGR through 2035. Australia ranks in the top 10 globally for HNWI populations, with over 42,000 individuals holding US$10M+ in assets and approximately 4,800 UHNWIs. The Daylesford/Hepburn Springs region is already recognised as Victoria's wellness capital — but there is no private members club or longevity-focused country retreat in the region.

The reference models map into a compelling hybrid: Long Lane (alcohol-free wellness hotel + members club), Saint Haven (urban wellness clubs with 15,000-person waitlists), Hampton (curated peer networking for founders/CEOs), and Lon Retreat (farm-based luxury retreat with farm-to-plate dining). No one in Australia has combined all four pillars into a rural, biodynamic, longevity-focused country club. That's the white space.

The target property — Lyonbank Falls — is a 28-hectare country estate with an architecturally transformed main house, 16 hectares of productive agricultural land on rich red volcanic soil, a spectacular waterfall, and existing infrastructure. Hepburn Shire Council adopted the Rural Hepburn Strategy in September 2024 explicitly supporting appropriate rural tourism. The Victorian Government released "Growing Victoria's Wellness Tourism Sector" guidance in August 2025.

Step 5

Risks & Mitigations

22 risks identified across 6 categories. None rated Critical. The phased fundraising structure is itself the primary risk mitigation — only $1.2M is deployed before the critical council approval gate.

Top 4 Risks (Scored 6/9)

Council approval refused

Phased fundraising caps pre-approval risk at $1.2M. Agri-tourism framing. Pre-application council engagement. Experienced local planner.

Planning delays

Budget for 12-month timeline. Parallel non-approval work: biodynamic conversion, brand, content, member recruitment.

Cash flow timing mismatch

Phased raises sized for full development. $2.5M founding member fees collected during build. Debt facility as buffer.

Medical regulatory requirements

Specialist health law firm from inception. Early Medical Director appointment. Phased clinical rollout — launch with lower-risk modalities.
