Executive Summary

ALUM represents a rare opportunity to develop a luxury biodynamic longevity country club on a pristine 28-hectare estate in regional Victoria. The development combines destination dining, experiential wellness, and premium accommodation within a working agricultural estate framework. This is a $7.8M capital deployment over 18 months to create a $3.16M p.a. revenue-generating asset targeting affluent, time-affluent guests seeking restorative experiences in nature.

The project leverages proven models (Brae, luxury wellness retreats, agritourism) with innovative modular architecture (Arcopod) to accelerate delivery while maintaining aesthetic coherence. The estate's existing infrastructure, heritage homestead, and natural assets (Lyonbank Falls, biodynamic soils, treeline habitat) reduce development risk and enhance positioning.

At steady state, ALUM targets 63% cabin occupancy ($750 blended ADR), generating $1.72M p.a. in accommodation revenue, plus $1.44M p.a. from the 50-seat Brae-inspired restaurant. Combined with ancillary services, total revenue reaches $3.16M p.a. with 38% breakeven occupancy, providing strong downside protection. The development aligns with Victorian wellness tourism strategy, Hepburn Shire agritourism positioning, and premium regional hospitality trends.

The Opportunity

Market Context: Australian wellness tourism is a $14.7B sector growing at 10% CAGR, with regional Victoria capturing increasing share as affluent urbanites seek nature-based respite and longevity experiences. The Daylesford/Macedon region is Victoria's premier wellness destination, yet lacks luxury destination accommodation that combines premium dining, curated wellness programming, and architectural distinction.

White Space: Competitors like Fawkner Park (Trentham) and local farmstays operate at lower price points (<$300 ADR) or lack dining/wellness depth. ALUM targets the $750+ ADR segment—couples and small groups with 7+ night stays, annual household income >$250K, seeking Instagram-worthy, Michelin-track dining experiences and structured wellness activities (yoga, foraging, biodynamic education).

Regional Positioning: Glenlyon is 90 minutes from Melbourne CBD, perfectly positioned within the "long weekend" geography. The estate's working biodynamic status and proximity to Lyonbank Falls creates authenticity competitors cannot replicate. Hepburn Shire's Rural Tourism Strategy explicitly supports premium agritourism, and Victorian planning reform enables agri-tourism development on farming-zoned land.

The Estate

The 28-hectare property at Lyonbank Falls is anchored by a 1960s heritage homestead, working biodynamic agricultural systems, mature treeline habitat, and natural water features. The estate combines operational farming zones with development-ready precincts, maximizing both authenticity and economics.

Development Principles

  • Biodynamic Integrity: Maintain working agricultural systems; preserve soil health and native habitat corridors.
  • Architectural Coherence: Modular design respects agrarian vernacular; dark exteriors recede into treeline.
  • Low-Impact Infrastructure: Biological treatment systems, renewable energy, permeable surfaces.
  • Experiential Authenticity: Guests engage with farming operations, foraging, seasonal rhythms.
  • Landscape Integration: 2,000+ native plantings, path networks, creek restoration.
  • Planning Compliance: Agri-tourism framing; Hepburn Shire Rural Tourism Strategy alignment.

Infrastructure & Systems

Guest Carparking

25–30 vehicle spaces with EV charging provisions. Permeable gravel surface (stormwater management); native screening hedgerows; positioned 200m+ from cabin viewsheds to maintain immersion. Includes accessible parking and coach bus bay for events.

Water & Waste Systems

Primary water supply from existing bore + seasonal creek harvesting (licensed allocation). Dual biological treatment system: greywater via constructed wetland (landscape irrigation); blackwater via anaerobic digester (nutrient recycling for biodynamic fields). Annual consumption target: <60L per guest per night (vs. industry average 150L).

Energy & Sustainability

Net-zero target via solar PV array expansion (16–20kW capacity), underfloor heating integration, LED throughout, and demand-response battery storage (30–50kWh). Gas booster for hot water in high-demand periods. Annual consumption: <30kWh per guest per night. All cabins designed to Passive House standard for thermal efficiency.

Connectivity & Digital

NBN fibre-to-the-premises infrastructure. High-speed WiFi mesh network across all precincts. Guest management via cloud PMS with mobile check-in. Smart environmental controls (temperature, lighting, water) accessible via app or manual override.

Landscaping & Environmental

2,000+ native plantings establishing biodiversity corridors and visual screening. Existing mature treeline retained; new plantings include understorey shrubs and groundcover species endemic to Central Victorian grassy woodlands. Dedicated maintenance pathways; seasonal creek access for guest interpretation programs. Long-term land stewardship integrates habitat restoration with operational farming.

Planning & Regulatory Alignment

Zoning & Agri-Tourism Framework

The property is zoned Farming Zone under Hepburn Shire planning scheme. Agri-tourism use (destination accommodation + dining) is permitted within the Farming Zone, subject to planning approval, provided the operation maintains agricultural credentials. ALUM's biodynamic farming operations, on-site food production, and landscape stewardship satisfy "agricultural use" criteria under Victorian planning guidelines.

Hepburn Shire Rural Tourism Strategy Alignment

Hepburn Shire's Rural Tourism Strategy (2019) explicitly supports premium agritourism developments that:

  • Leverage regional identity (biodynamic credentials, Macedon Ranges heritage)
  • Create employment and economic benefits for regional communities
  • Maintain environmental and landscape values
  • Support local food and beverage producers

ALUM aligns with all four pillars through destination restaurant positioning, local supply chain emphasis, biodiversity stewardship, and regional employment (15–20 FTE).

Victorian Wellness Tourism Policy

Victoria's Wellness Tourism Action Plan (2019–2024) prioritizes regional destination development and premium positioning. ALUM supports state tourism targets by creating a high-yield, low-impact regional asset within the priority Macedon Ranges cluster.

Environmental & Heritage Considerations

Biodiversity assessments will confirm habitat value and identify plantings to enhance native species corridors. Heritage assessment of shearing shed will guide adaptive reuse approach. Archaeological survey will precede any ground disturbance. All works comply with Aboriginal cultural heritage protocols and Flora & Fauna Guarantee Act obligations.
